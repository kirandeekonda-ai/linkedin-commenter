The prompt-to-app workflow is incredibly effective for rapid prototyping. Describing a feature and deploying it instantly makes building early prototypes faster than ever.

However, a serious architectural wall is hit the moment you attempt to graduate these generative demos into enterprise production. 

Most prompt-to-app builders deploy code directly onto their own managed clouds, not yours. For a standalone experiment, this model is fine. But for enterprise-grade engineering, it introduces major operational challenges:

1. **Zero Observability**: You lose native integration with enterprise logging and monitoring systems (like Datadog, Sentry, or custom telemetry).
2. **Broken Integration Pipelines**: The generated app cannot be easily integrated into your existing CI/CD pipelines or tested against internal staging environments.
3. **Compliance and Security Blockers**: Getting security teams to sign off on a vendor-controlled runtime is nearly impossible when handling SOC 2 compliance, GDPR, or sensitive data.

This is why the conversation around Bring Your Own Cloud (BYOC) in the generative space is so critical. 

If you are evaluating AI code-generation tools for production-grade software, we must look beyond prompt-to-deployment speed. The true bottleneck is deployment control and architectural auditability.

How is your team balancing the speed of instant-generation platforms against the requirement for cloud-agnostic, self-hosted infrastructure? Let's discuss.
